Q: How quickly should I respond to a negative Google review?
A: Aim to respond within 24 hours. A prompt response shows you are proactive and care about customer feedback. A delayed response can make the situation seem ignored or unimportant to future readers.

Q: Is it okay to offer a discount or free item in exchange for a Google review?
A: No. Google's policies strictly prohibit offering incentives in exchange for reviews. You can ask happy customers to leave an honest review, but you cannot condition a reward on a positive rating. Focus on providing a great experience and making it easy for them to share their genuine feedback.

Q: What's the best way to get more photos in our Google reviews?
A: Train your staff to politely encourage it. Servers can say, "That dish looks amazing! If you're sharing on Google, a photo would be awesome." You can also run a low-key contest, like featuring the best customer photo of the month on your social media.

Q: Can I delete a bad Google review?
A: You cannot delete a review yourself. You can only report it to Google for removal if it violates Google's policies (e.g., contains hate speech, is spam, or is off-topic). For legitimate critical reviews, your only recourse is to respond professionally and try to resolve the issue offline.

Q: How many new reviews should my restaurant aim for each month?
A: A good target is 10-20 genuine reviews per month for a single location. Consistency is more important than a huge spike. A steady flow of recent reviews signals an active, popular business to both customers and Google's algorithm.
