Q: What is the single most effective way to ask for a Google review?
A: The most effective method combines optimal timing with zero friction. Sending a personalized SMS with a direct review link 2-3 hours after a positive customer experience consistently yields the highest conversion rates. It's timely, convenient, and removes all barriers to posting.

Q: Is it against Google's rules to offer a discount for a review?
A: Yes, it is a direct violation of Google's policies. You cannot offer any incentive—money, free items, discounts, or contest entries—in exchange for posting a review on Google. You can offer incentives for checking in on social media or for providing private feedback directly to you.

Q: How can I get a link that takes customers straight to writing a review?
A: You need your Google Business Profile's direct review link. You can find this in your profile manager. Since this link is often long and messy, always use a free link shortener like Bitly to create a clean, memorable link (e.g., bit.ly/YourRestaurant-Review) to use in all your communications.

Q: My staff is uncomfortable asking for reviews. What should I do?
A: Frame it as "helping other customers" rather than "getting praise." Provide them with simple, non-pushy scripts (like the ones in this guide) and role-play the conversation. Start by having only managers ask for a week to demonstrate how natural and positive it can be. Celebrate when a review comes in from their table.

Q: How many reviews should I aim to get per month?
A: Quality and consistency matter more than a specific number. A realistic starting goal for a busy restaurant is 10-20 new, authentic reviews per month. This shows Google and customers consistent, recent engagement. Focus on building a sustainable system rather than a one-time push.
