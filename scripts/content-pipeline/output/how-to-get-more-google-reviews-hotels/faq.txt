Q: What is the single best time to ask a hotel guest for a Google review?
A: The most effective moment is during the check-out process at the front desk. The guest's experience is complete and fresh in their mind, and an in-person, polite ask from a staff member has the highest conversion rate, far outperforming post-stay emails.

Q: Where should I place QR codes in my hotel to get more reviews?
A: The highest-converting locations are on the back of the guest room door and on a stand at the front desk next to the payment terminal. Other effective spots include on the key card sleeve, in elevators, and printed on the final checkout folio.

Q: How can I get reviews from international guests who speak different languages?
A: Use a review collection system that can present the request in the guest's detected language. When responding to reviews in other languages, use translation tools to understand them, but have a native speaker vet or write the response. A reply in the guest's language shows exceptional service.

Q: Should I focus on Google reviews or OTA (like Booking.com) reviews?
A: You must manage both, but prioritize growing your Google reviews. Google reviews impact your local search visibility for all travelers, not just those on a specific OTA site, and they help drive more profitable direct bookings. A good strategy is to respond to positive OTA reviews and politely invite those guests to also share their feedback on Google.

Q: How do I handle a negative Google review for my hotel?
A: Respond publicly, promptly, and professionally. Acknowledge the issue, apologize sincerely, explain any corrective steps taken, and invite the guest to continue the conversation offline via direct contact. A thoughtful response can actually build trust with future readers.
