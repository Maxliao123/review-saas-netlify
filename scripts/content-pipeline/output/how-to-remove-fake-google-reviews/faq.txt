Q: How long does it take Google to remove a fake review?
A: The review process can take anywhere from 3 to 10 business days, sometimes longer. Google does not provide a specific timeline. If the review is not removed after your first flag, you should report it again with any additional evidence you have.

Q: Can I pay to have a negative Google review removed?
A: No. Google does not offer a paid service to remove reviews. Any service claiming to do so is likely a scam. Removal only happens if the review violates Google's published content policies. The proper channel is the official flagging process.

Q: What if the fake reviewer is anonymous?
A: You can and should still report the review. Google can see more user data than is publicly displayed. Your report should focus on the content of the review (factual inaccuracies, policy violations) rather than the anonymity of the reviewer, as anonymous but legitimate reviews are allowed.

Q: Should I respond to a fake review before it's removed?
A: Yes, in most cases. A calm, professional public response shows other customers you are engaged and casts doubt on the review's authenticity. Frame your response to address future readers, not the fake reviewer, stating you've investigated and found no record of the experience and have reported the review.

Q: Can a competitor be penalized for posting fake reviews about my business?
A: Yes. Posting fake reviews is a violation of Google's policies and, in some jurisdictions, may be illegal under fair competition laws. If you have strong evidence (like a paper trail or admission), you can report the competitor's behavior to Google and consult with an attorney about potential legal action.
