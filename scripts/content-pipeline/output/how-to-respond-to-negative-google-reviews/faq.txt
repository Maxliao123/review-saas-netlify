Q: How long should my response to a negative Google review be?
A: Keep it between 75-150 words. It should be long enough to show empathy, apologize, and outline a resolution path, but concise enough to be easily read. Avoid lengthy justifications or arguments.

Q: Should I offer a refund in the public response?
A: No. State that you have taken or will take action to make it right, but never specify the exact compensation (e.g., "I've issued a full refund") publicly. Invite the customer to contact you privately to discuss the resolution.

Q: What if the customer never replies to my attempt to take the conversation offline?
A: You've done your part. Your public response shows future customers that you acknowledged the issue and tried to resolve it. You can add a brief, polite follow-up comment after a few days ("Just following up on my message below"), but then let it rest.

Q: Is it okay to ask a customer to update or remove their negative review after resolving the issue?
A: You can politely ask if they would *consider* updating their review to reflect the resolved outcome. Phrase it as a request, not a demand. Never offer an incentive (money, freebies) in exchange for altering a review, as this violates Google's policies.

Q: How do I handle fake or competitor reviews?
A: Flag them as "Spam or fake content" directly in your Google Business Profile. If Google doesn't remove it, you can post a single, factual response stating, "We have no record of serving this customer on the date mentioned. We believe this review may be inaccurate." This alerts future readers without engaging in an argument.
